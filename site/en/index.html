Sailing

Round the world

After four years from the purchase of FSM, 21.600 nautical miles (equivalent to the entire earth circumference), many improvements, many friends, new and old, on the 1st of September 2024 I've started my westbound voyage. Let's see how it goes Have a look to the map and to the photos if you're curious.

What I have done so far:

  • The Mediterranean and migrants
  • Gibraltar, the orcas
  • The former Spanish Sahara: cross-colonizations
  • Mauritania, the land of inhospitability
  • Senegal, the Saloum Delta and humpback dolphins
  • Cape Verde, volcanoes and cocaine
  • Noronha, the reserve of wealthy Brazilians
  • Brazil, such lightness!
  • Guyana, between Europe and Vietnam
  • Guna Yala, fractal independence
  • The great crossing, from Easter to Easter
  • The island of pirates and the queen, Pitcairn
  • French Polynesia, the myth: Tahiti, Tuamotu, Gambier
  • Paradise exists: the Marquesas Islands
  • Kiritimati, the island of paradoxes

Sailing together

I'm happy sailing alone, but being together can be more pleasant, sometimes less tiresome, and safer.
I try her to explain my vision of sharing sailing time for those interested to come and sail on FSM; if it's compatible with yours, you're welcome on board.

What I offer

A sailing experience as free as possible, according to the weather, the specific program and the desires: sometimes they can be long crossings without stopping, other times short hops and swimming in a bay.
Have a look to comments by people who sailed with me and to the map of my sailing trips.
I will gladly share what I have learned if you wish. I will gladly learn from those who know more than me.

What I ask

Respect for the boat and attention to keep it in the best conditions.
Help in navigation, in relation to your marine experience and expertise.
Take charge of the food: buy it and prepare meals, and the occasional tavern.
Better not to smoke on board.
A significant participation of boat maintenance costs.

Please note

OVNI 32 is a bluewater boat, seaworty and robust, but comparatively demanding and basic. She has two private cabins, two additional berths (one single, one double), and one head.
You're all welcome. Safety and good living on board are a priority.

Sustainable sailing

I believe sailing is a fantastic way to live more sustainably, and I make any reasonable effort to keep it so:

  • FSM is made of aluminium, a highly reciclabe material, contrary to various resins
  • I use the engine as little as possible, only for maneuvers (and not always) and when conditions are otherwise dangerous or cause excessive damage to the equipment; I consume on average less than a liter of diesel per day
  • I use solar energy to recharge the batteries and heat the water
  • I limit consumption to the minimum from all points of view (water, energy, chemicals, vegetarian & vegan friendly etc.) reducing the equipment to the minimum necessary to ensure safety; I believe that reducing the impact on the environment is more a question of reduction than of complex technological solutions (as long as possible, feet are more sustainable than a Tesla)
